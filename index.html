TREES

by: MARK ONG

I
think
that     I
Shall never
see,   A poem
Lovely  as a tree
A tree  whose hungry
mount is priest,  Against
the   earth's sweet   flowing
breast.   A tree   that   looks at
God all day,   And lifts her leaft
arms to pray,   A tree   that may in
Summer wear.   A nest   of robins in
her     hait , upon whose bosom   snow
has lain,   whose intimately live with rain
poems are make
by fools like me,
but only God can
make   a   tree.
